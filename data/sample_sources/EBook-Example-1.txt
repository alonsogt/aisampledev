CHAPTER 1: Why Your AI Strategy Starts with Data, Not Models
-------------------------------------------------------------
Three years ago, TechForward's CTO told us she had a problem. Her team had spent
$400,000 on an AI platform and gotten almost nothing useful out of it. The models
were fine. The data was a disaster.

This is the most common mistake mid-market companies make when starting their AI
journey: they buy the brain before they've built the nervous system.

In this chapter, we'll show you how to audit your data readiness in one afternoon
and create a 90-day roadmap to fix the gaps that matter most.

Key Takeaways:
- AI success is 80% data quality and 20% model quality.
- A data audit takes one afternoon, not three months.
- Fix data pipelines before buying AI platforms.
- The "good enough" data threshold is lower than most teams think.
- Start with one high-value use case, not a platform-wide rollout.

CHAPTER 2: Choosing the Right Model for Your Use Case
------------------------------------------------------
Not every problem needs GPT-4. In fact, for most structured business tasks —
classification, extraction, routing — a smaller, faster, cheaper model will
outperform a large foundation model on your specific data.

The key is matching model complexity to task complexity.

We use a simple 2x2 matrix: task complexity (low/high) vs. data availability
(low/high). Each quadrant points to a different model strategy.

Low complexity + high data: fine-tune a small model. Fast, cheap, accurate.
High complexity + low data: use a large foundation model with few-shot prompting.
Low complexity + low data: rules-based system first. Add AI later.
High complexity + high data: full fine-tuning or RAG pipeline.

Key Takeaways:
- Match model size to task complexity, not to budget.
- Fine-tuned small models beat large models on narrow tasks.
- RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) is the sweet spot for most enterprise use cases.
- Always benchmark on your own data before committing to a model.

CHAPTER 3: Building Your AI Governance Policy
----------------------------------------------
The companies that will win with AI in the next five years are not the ones
moving fastest. They are the ones moving fastest within guardrails.

An AI governance policy does not slow you down. Done right, it is the thing
that lets your team move faster — because every decision doesn't need to go
through a committee.

A minimal viable governance policy covers four areas:
1. Permitted use cases (what AI can be used for)
2. Data handling rules (what data can be fed into AI systems)
3. Output review requirements (which outputs need human review before action)
4. Incident response (what happens when something goes wrong)

You can write version 1.0 in an afternoon. We'll show you how.
