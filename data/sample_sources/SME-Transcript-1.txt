SME INTERVIEW TRANSCRIPT
Subject: AI Content Strategy for Professional Services Firms
Interviewer: Bridge Partners Research Team
Subject Matter Expert: Director of Content, Global Consulting Firm (anonymized)
Date: January 2025
=================================================================================

INTERVIEWER: Tell us about where your content team was before you started using AI.

SME: We had a team of twelve writers producing content for six service lines.
Everything was bespoke — every white paper, every case study, every blog post
written from scratch. We were slow, expensive, and inconsistent. The same topic
could be covered in completely different tones by two different writers.

INTERVIEWER: What was the first thing you changed?

SME: We built a prompt library before we touched any AI tool. That sounds boring,
but it was the most important thing we did. We documented exactly what good looked
like for each content type — white paper, case study, social post, email — with
examples of our best and worst work. That became the foundation for every prompt
we wrote.

INTERVIEWER: How long did that take?

SME: About three weeks for the first version. We've been refining it ever since.
It's now our most valuable internal document. New writers learn our voice faster
from the prompt library than from any other onboarding material.

INTERVIEWER: What were the early failures?

SME: We tried to use AI for case studies first. That was a mistake. Case studies
need specific client details, specific outcomes, specific quotes. AI kept making
things up or being too generic. We pulled back and started with thought leadership
instead, where the ideas matter more than the specifics.

INTERVIEWER: What does your workflow look like now?

SME: A strategist writes a brief — about 400 words covering the audience, the
key argument, the three supporting points, the tone, and the CTA. That brief
goes to the AI, which produces a 1,500-word draft in about 90 seconds. A senior
editor spends 30–45 minutes revising. We fact-check anything specific. Then
it goes to the subject matter expert for a final 15-minute review.

Total time per piece: about two hours versus eight hours before.

INTERVIEWER: What do your writers think?

SME: Mixed at first. Some felt threatened. But once they saw that their job
became about strategy and quality — not typing — most came around. We actually
promoted two writers to "AI Content Strategist" roles. They now own the prompt
library and train the rest of the team. It became a career opportunity.

INTERVIEWER: What's the single most important piece of advice you'd give?

SME: Don't start with the tool. Start with the brief. If you can't write a
good brief, AI will produce mediocre content at scale. If you can write a
great brief, AI will produce great content at scale. The brief is the leverage.
The AI is just the multiplier.
