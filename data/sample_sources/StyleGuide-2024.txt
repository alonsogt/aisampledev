BRIDGE PARTNERS — CONTENT STYLE GUIDE 2024
==========================================

BRAND VOICE
-----------
Bridge Partners writes in a confident, direct, and human tone.
We are experts, but we never talk down to our audience.
We use plain English over jargon wherever possible.
We are precise: say exactly what you mean in as few words as needed.

TONE GUIDELINES
---------------
- Professional but approachable — not stiff or corporate.
- Use "you" and "we" to create a conversational feel.
- Avoid passive voice. Prefer active constructions.
- Short sentences land harder. Use them for key points.
- Longer sentences are fine for context and nuance.

HEADINGS
--------
- Title Case for H1 and H2 headings.
- Sentence case for H3 and below.
- Headings should describe the value the reader gets, not just the topic.
  Good: "How to Cut Report Time in Half"
  Avoid: "Reporting Methodology"

TERMINOLOGY
-----------
- Use "AI agent" not "bot" or "chatbot" for autonomous systems.
- Use "workflow" not "process flow" in client-facing content.
- Capitalize "AI" always. Never "A.I." or "Ai."
- "Data" is plural: "data are" not "data is" in formal writing.
- Refer to the reader's organization as "your team" not "the organization."

FORMATTING RULES
----------------
- Bullet lists: 3–7 items max. More than 7 means split into sections.
- Bold for the most important word or phrase in a sentence, not whole sentences.
- Avoid underlining — it looks like a hyperlink.
- Oxford comma is required: "strategy, execution, and measurement."
- Numbers: spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above.
- Percentages: always use the % symbol with a numeral (e.g., 42%).

E-BOOK SPECIFIC RULES
---------------------
- Chapter titles should be action-oriented or question-based.
  Good: "Building Your First AI Governance Policy"
  Avoid: "AI Governance Overview"
- Each chapter should open with a 2–3 sentence hook — a story, stat, or bold claim.
- Include at least one concrete example per major section.
- End every chapter with a "Key Takeaways" box of 3–5 bullets.
- Avoid ending a chapter on a question — end on a forward-looking statement.

WHAT TO AVOID
-------------
- Buzzwords without meaning: "synergy," "leverage" (as a verb), "paradigm shift."
- Hedging language: "kind of," "sort of," "maybe," "it might be argued."
- Walls of text — break up paragraphs every 3–4 sentences.
- Starting consecutive sentences with "The."
