EBOOK CHAPTER TEMPLATE — BRIDGE PARTNERS STANDARD
===================================================

USE THIS STRUCTURE FOR EVERY CHAPTER
--------------------------------------

[CHAPTER TITLE]
Action-oriented or question-based. 6–10 words max.
Example: "How to Build an AI Governance Policy in a Day"

[OPENING HOOK — 2-3 sentences]
Start with a story, a surprising statistic, or a bold claim.
Do NOT start with a definition. Do NOT start with "In today's world..."
Example: "Most AI governance policies take six months to write and never get used.
Here's how one team wrote one that actually works — in an afternoon."

[SECTION 1: THE PROBLEM / CONTEXT]
What challenge does this chapter address?
Keep it to 2-3 paragraphs. Be specific. Use real numbers if available.

[SECTION 2: THE FRAMEWORK / APPROACH]
What is the Bridge Partners recommended approach?
Use a numbered list, a 2x2 matrix, or a step-by-step breakdown.
Maximum 5 steps or items. More than 5 = split into two sections.

[SECTION 3: HOW TO APPLY IT]
Walk the reader through applying the framework to their situation.
Include at least one concrete example (real or composite/anonymized).
Use second person: "your team," "your data," "your use case."

[SECTION 4: COMMON MISTAKES]
2-3 bullets on what to avoid. Keep it punchy.
Format: "Don't [action] — [why it backfires]."

[KEY TAKEAWAYS BOX]
3-5 bullets. Start each with a verb.
These should be the most actionable points from the chapter.
Example:
- Audit your data before buying AI tools.
- Write your governance policy for one use case first, not all use cases.
- Assign a named owner to every AI system you deploy.

[BRIDGE TO NEXT CHAPTER — 1 sentence]
Forward-looking. What will the next chapter help them do?
Example: "Now that your governance foundation is in place, Chapter 4 shows
you how to select the right AI model for your first use case."
